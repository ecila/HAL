Registration

Registration will open mid May, 2016.

Registration fees will be kept low (above £80, but below £200): they will pay for lunches, coffee and tea, room rentals and other related costs of running an academic conference. There will be a student discount.

Registration fees include:
- Attendance to paper and poster sessions, concerts, workshops and keynote
- Conference guide and electronic proceedings
- Lunch and coffee on all the days

Accomodation is not included in conference fees, but it is possible to book rooms on campus with Residential Services. Here, the minimum stay is 7 nights (but prices as as low as £17 per night). A more appealing option might be to book accommodation through our partner Visit Brighton where you can find accommodation on campus and in the city. Yet another possibility is to find accommodation through the Bed and Breakfasts guide.

Brighton is a very popular destination with tourists in the summer, so booking early is a good idea.
